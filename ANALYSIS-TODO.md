## A. Intake and response-rate analyses (inputs committed)

1. **Response rate by share eligible.** DONE (2026-07-11): deck slide "Usable response rises only weakly with the LSOA's eligible share" (+ EB-dot companion in the appendix). WLS (letters weights): +0.5 usable per 1,000 letters per +10 pp of eligible share (robust SE 0.35, t = 1.5). Disadvantage pattern visible in the quintile coloring: high-share LSOAs skew deprived, where response is lower, so the two gradients offset. Map-test addendum (2026-07-12, deck slide "A fresher eligibility map would not have helped"): the near-flat gradient is not a stale-map artifact. The mid-2023/24 LSOA estimates were first published 2025-11-07, three weeks after the 2025-10-14 design freeze, so no fresher map existed at planning time. The gradient is behavioral, not cartographic.
2. **Back-of-envelope response rate per eligible household.** DONE (2026-07-11): deck slide "Response per (census-)eligible household falls as the eligible share rises". Level ~28 usable per 1,000 estimated eligible mailings at the mean; slope -11 per 1,000 per +10 pp (robust SE 2, t = -6.6) - the arithmetic mirror of item 1's flat raw gradient. 2b. **External check of the census eligible-share estimates against self-reported eligibility** (added 2026-07-10). DONE (2026-07-11; recut 2026-07-12): deck slide "Those who answer the eligibility question say yes ~92% of the time, everywhere". This check bears on the denominator construction for items 1-2 and on the Part 2 identification assumption. Its limitation must be stated wherever it is used: the comparison conditions on having responded, and eligible and ineligible households respond at different rates, so responder-reported eligibility does not estimate Pr(eligible | mailed) directly.

DONE (2026-07-11): estimation run by `ECLIPS-PAP/scripts/28-estimate-saturation-spillover.R`; results in `spillover-estimates.csv` (codebook alongside). Null in every prespecified specification: primary -1.0% (started; 95% CI -5.7% to +3.7%) and -1.1% (usable; CI -7.7% to +5.6%) per +3.5 pp of saturation; quasi-binomial dispersion 0.68 / 0.52. CONCLUSION REVISED (2026-07-11, Frank): the original power presentation hid the football by quoting MDEs per the design's own SD. In slope units the detectable dose-response is 2.1% / 2.9% relative per +1 pp of saturation - only an implausibly steep, hyper-local mechanism is detectable, because OAs (94-211 addresses) cap the chance variation near 3.5 pp SD. The null is therefore uninformative about plausible spillovers. The design note's MDE section was rewritten in place (ECLIPS-PAP commit ed58995); the deck carries a single appendix slide recording the exercise honestly ("checked; the data cannot answer it"). Worth at most a footnote in the paper.
